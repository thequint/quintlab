  • Dumna Nature Park

    Dumna Nature Park

    Spread across 1058 hectares, this ecotourism spot boasts of exotic flora and fauna. The nature park is home to a number of wild animals including chital, wild boar and jackals.

  • Rani Durgavati Museum

    Rani Durgavati Museum

    Built in memory of Queen Durgavati, this museum houses sculptures pertaining to the unique Kalachuri art. This museum also has some letters and photographs related to Mahatma Gandhi.

  • Tilwara Ghat

    Tilwara Ghat

    Situated on the banks of Narmada, Tilwara Ghat has great religious significance and is also the place where Mahatma Gandhi’s ashes were immersed. The Mahatma also stayed at the city’s Beohar Niwas Palace while visiting Jabalpur during the freedom struggle.

  • Bhedaghat Marble Rocks

    Bhedaghat Marble Rocks

    Magnificent mountains of marble that flank the serene Narmada on either side and rise up to a 100 feet. The Bhedaghat Marble Rocks are beautiful during the day, but look absolutely spellbinding in the moonlight.

  • Madan Mahal

    Madan Mahal

    This enchanting fort is situated atop a hill and offers you a stunning view of the city. It was built in the 8th century by Gond rulers. The fort is associated with Gond queen Rani Durgavati and her son Madan Singh, after whom it is named.

  • Dhuandhar Falls

    Dhuandhar Falls

    Located in Jabalpur district, this waterfall derives its name from the words dhuan (smoke) and dhar (flow). This is because as the waters of the Narmada fall from a height of nearly 98 feet, they resemble a cloud of smoke.

  • Chausath Yogini Temple

    Chausath Yogini Temple

    This 10th century temple is one of the oldest heritage sites in India. It has a steep flight of 108 stairs and a massive diameter of 125 feet. This temple is the abode of Goddess Durga and 64 yoginis, hence the name 'Chausath Yogini’.

  • Pisanhari Ki Madiya

    Pisanhari Ki Madiya

    This is a popular Jain pilgrimage centre and is nearly 500 years old. Legend has it that it was built by a poor lady who ground flour, which is what ‘pisanhari’ means.

  • Birthplace of Snooker

    Birthplace of Snooker

    Trivia enthusiasts, here’s one for your next quiz. It is believed that the game of snooker was conceived in Jabalpur’s British Army Officer’s Mess by Colonel Sir Neville Chamberlain in 1875.
